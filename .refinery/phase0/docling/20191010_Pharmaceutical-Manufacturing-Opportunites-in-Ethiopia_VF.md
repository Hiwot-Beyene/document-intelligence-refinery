## Large &amp; growing population and rising health care coverage make pharmaceutical manufacturing an attractive endeavor

## Key drivers of pharmaceutical demand in Ethiopia

- Second-largest population in Africa (&gt;100M, ~8.5% of Africa's population ); growth of 2.3% per year is expected
- With average economic growth of &gt;10% per year, increase in disposable income will influence spending on pharmaceutical products
- Increasing healthcare coverage by government to the large rural population creates growing demand for pharmaceutical products.
- Primary health care service coverage reached 100% in 2015
- Awareness, diagnosis and treatment are increasing
- High burden of communicable diseases: malaria, HIV/AIDS, TB
- Prevalence of non-communicable diseases: diabetes, cancer, hypertension

## Fiscal Incentives tailored to investment

Exemption from income tax up to 8 - 10 years for enterprises inside IPs,

Exemption from duties and other taxes on imports of machinery, equipment, construction materials, spare parts, raw materials and vehicles

Loss carry forward

Full export duty exemption
